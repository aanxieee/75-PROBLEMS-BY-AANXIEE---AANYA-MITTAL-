MyFitnessPal thinks a "roti" has 300 calories. 🫠

That's because most nutrition apps are built on Western food databases — where a "wrap" and a "chapati" are apparently the same thing.

So for Day 8 of my 75 Hard Engineer Edition, I built an Indian Diet Nutrition Tracker that actually understands our food.

Here's what it does:
→ 74 curated Indian foods (dal, roti, paneer, dahi, sabzi — real portion sizes)
→ Tracks 12 nutrients: macros + calcium, iron, zinc, vitamins A/B12/C/D
→ Personalized targets using Mifflin-St Jeor equation (your age, weight, goal)
→ Smart suggestions in Hinglish: "Protein kam hai — paneer ya dal lo 💪"
→ SQLite persistence — your data stays across sessions

The AI twist? Rule-based intelligence that analyzes your current meal pattern and tells you exactly what to eat next. Not generic advice — specific to YOUR deficit.

Built for a real problem I face daily: main kya khaun jo calories deficit mein rahe aur nutrition bhi poora ho?

Tech: Streamlit + SQLite + Custom Indian Food Database
Domain: Healthcare | Level: Production-ready Streamlit app
